How do You See

Light bounces off of objects into your eyes.
The light then gets focused by your lens and gets absorbed by your rods and cones in your eyes.
These signals are then interperated by your brain and color is percived.
Here is a relatively simple model of human color perception.
The sliders on the left choose what color is being transmitted into the eye.
The sliders on the right represent the amount of rods and cones of each type.
The color gets broken down into the activation of each then reconstructed back into a color.
This is not fully accurate as the combination here is linear and actual vision is not.

What Cant You See

Not all combinations of rods and cones creates a valid color.
An example of these are something called chimera colors.
Chimera Colors are the combination of two colors that have no additive mix.

How Far do You See?

Depth perceiption is your ability to see how far away an object is.
There are a few ways that this is achieved.
One method is stereoscopic vision.
This where mind is able to use the differences between the left and right eye
Below is an example of stereoscopic 3D using a stereoscopy. To see the effect cross your eyes like in the color example.
Another way you are able to sense depth is based on context.
We have an idea that certain obejcts are a specific size. This hints your mind with how large objects should be relative to eachother and implies a depth.

Top Down / Bottom Up

Bottom Up refers to mental processes that feed up into into higher level functions.
For vision that would would be visual data being processed then fed up into feature recognion then into object recognion.

Top Down are processes that start higher level and feed down into lower mental processing.
This would be the recognion of what the object is and feed down into sensory recognition.
Arrows to the left representing top down processing.
Arrows to the right representing bottom up processing.

As implied from context based depth perceiption, human vision works as a combination of top down and bottom up processes.
